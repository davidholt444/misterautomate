LinkedIn Feed Filter

Cut the noise. See what matters.

A browser extension that filters your LinkedIn feed so you only see posts from people you actually care about.

LinkedIn's algorithm fills your feed with sponsored content, viral posts from strangers, and activity from connections you'd rather not follow. This filter puts you back in control.

Download the extension

How it works

The LinkedIn Feed Filter is a browser extension that runs locally in Chrome or Edge. It hides feed items that don't match your criteria, leaving only the posts that are relevant to you.

  • Filters out promoted and sponsored posts
  • Hides suggested posts from people you don't follow
  • Removes activity from connections you want to mute
  • Runs entirely in your browser — no data leaves your machine

Should you trust a random browser extension?

Fair question. Browser extensions run with elevated permissions and you have no reason to take a stranger's word for it.

So don't. Verify it yourself.

Download the zip, then upload it directly to your favorite AI — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, whatever you use — and ask it to review the code. Something like:

"I'm about to install this browser extension. Can you read through all the files and tell me exactly what it does, whether it's safe to install, and whether there's anything suspicious or malicious?"

Every line of code in the extension is readable plain text. An LLM can review the entire thing in seconds and give you a straight answer. If anything looks off, don't install it.

Installation

  1. Download and unzip the file above.
  2. Open Chrome or Edge and go to chrome://extensions (or edge://extensions).
  3. Enable Developer mode using the toggle in the top right.
  4. Click Load unpacked and select the unzipped folder.
  5. Navigate to LinkedIn — the filter will activate automatically.
